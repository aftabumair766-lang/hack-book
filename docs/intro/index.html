About This Coursebook‚Äã

This coursebook bridges the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application in the rapidly evolving field of Physical AI. It is designed for:

  • University students (undergraduate and graduate) studying robotics, AI, or mechatronics
  • Academic researchers exploring embodied intelligence and human-robot interaction
  • Professional robotics engineers seeking to deepen their knowledge of modern robotics systems

What is Physical AI?‚Äã

Physical AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that interact with and operate in the physical world. Unlike virtual AI systems that exist purely in digital environments, Physical AI encompasses:

  • Embodied Intelligence: AI that learns through physical interaction with its environment
  • Perception: Sensing and interpreting the physical world through sensors
  • Motion & Control: Planning and executing physical movements
  • Adaptation: Learning from physical experiences to improve performance

Why Physical AI Matters‚Äã

The future of AI is not just in data centers and cloud platforms ‚Äì it's in robots that can:

  • Assist in healthcare and elderly care
  • Work alongside humans in manufacturing
  • Explore dangerous or remote environments
  • Perform complex manipulation tasks
  • Interact naturally with humans
